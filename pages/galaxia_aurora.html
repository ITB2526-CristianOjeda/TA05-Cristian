Aurora Galaxy

Interactive Universe Explorer • By Jesús & Cristian

What is this project about?

Aurora Galaxy is an interactive map of the universe that allows users to explore fictional galaxies, nebulae, stars, and planets. The project mixes real astronomical data with imagined worlds, generating an educational and visually spectacular experience.

It includes star routes, planet fact sheets, hyperspace travel animations, and a navigable 3D environment accessible from any modern browser.

Why does it stand out?

This project turns learning astronomy into a game. It allows you to visually travel through the galaxy as if you were a real explorer aboard a spaceship.
It stands out for its smooth and optimized graphics, running even on modest devices, thus fostering scientific curiosity and creativity in those who visit.

Origin: Inspired by the James Webb

Jesús and Cristian started the project after being fascinated by images from the James Webb Telescope. Inspired by the ultra-detailed photos of nebulae, they decided to build their own interactive version of the universe.

They started by designing just 3 stars, but their ambition grew, and they expanded the map to over 40 star systems. What began as a small experiment ended up becoming a full project with 3D graphics and exploration tools.

The Positives

  • Highly visual, immersive, and striking.
  • Easy to use for anyone.
  • Information based on real astronomy concepts.
  • Ideal for education and entertainment.

Areas for Improvement

  • Requires a modern browser for 3D features.
  • Limited accessibility for the visually impaired.
  • Missing details on some distant planets.
